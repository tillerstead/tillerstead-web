**Inherits From**: `SYSTEM.md`  
**Scope**: All marketing copy, service descriptions, blog posts, and
client-facing content

strategy

---

## CORE VOICE DEFINITION

### Primary Voice: The TCNA-Literate Professional (No Nonsense, No Corners Cut)

- **Technically Authoritative, Legally Compliant**: Use TCNA, ANSI, New Jersey
  HIC, and OSHA terminology; explain for client clarity
- **Confident, Never Condescending**: Empower clients with knowledge, not ego
- **Self-Aware Humor**: Acknowledge our meticulousness—never at the client’s
  expense
- **Direct, Honest, Transparent**: State facts, cite standards, own limitations

## LANGUAGE PATTERNS

- **Power Words**: Documented, Tested, Standards-Compliant, Licensed
  (#13VH10808800), Verified, Accountable, Realistic, Preventive
- **Humor**: Self-deprecating pedantry, absurdist comparisons, exaggerated
  obviousness, sarcastic air quotes, parenthetical asides
- **Technical Specificity**: Always pair terms with implications  
   _“L/360 deflection (so your tile doesn’t crack)”_
- **Contrast**:  
   _“Schluter KERDI membrane, not red paint from the box store”_  
   _“Texts returned same-day, not left on read”_

---

## SENTENCE STRUCTURE

- **Headlines**: Bold, technical, or provocative  
   _“Built to Last. Installed with Judgment.”_
- **Body Copy**: Short punch, technical detail, payoff/humor  
   _“Your floor isn’t flat. We measure deflection (L/360), check substrate
  (1/8\" in 10'), and find moisture issues before they’re surprises.”_
- **Bullets**:  
   _**Waterproof showers that don’t leak**: Schluter KERDI, flood tested, seam
  details correct. Because ‘probably fine’ isn’t waterproofing._

## CONTENT-SPECIFIC GUIDELINES

- **Service Pages**:  
   Bold claim → Technical specifics → Contrast → Client benefit
- **About/Process**:  
   Personality → Technical competence → Process clarity → CTA
- **Blog Posts**:  
   Common question → Technical answer → Practical implication → DIY vs. pro
- **Social Media**:  
   Progress photos, technical captions, before/after, “things we find”

---

## RED LINES (NEVER CROSS)

- **Don’t**:  
   Name competitors, use fear-mongering, overpromise, disparage DIY, use
  unexplained jargon, be negative without solutions, compromise safety, mock
  homeowners
- **Do**:  
   Contrast methods, address concerns with solutions, set realistic
  expectations, offer to assess/repair DIY, explain terms, point out problems
  _and_ solutions, take safety seriously, educate for informed decisions
